Job Prepares His Case

1“Indeed, my eyes have seen all this;

my ears have heard and understood.

2What you know, I also know;

I am not inferior to you.

3Yet I desire to speak to the Almightyalmighty

and argue my case before God.

4You, however, smear with lies;

you are all worthless physicians.

5If only you would remain silent;

for that would be your wisdom!

6Hear now my argument,

and listen to the plea of my lips.

7Will you speak wickedly on God’s behalf

or speak deceitfully for Himhim?

8Would you show Himhim partiality

or argue in Hishis defencedefencedefense?

9Would it be well when Hehe examined you?

Could you deceive Himhim like a man?

10Surely Hehe would rebuke you

if you secretly showed partiality.

11Would Hishis majesty not terrify you?

Would the dread of Himhim not fall upon you?

12Your maxims are proverbs of ashes;

your defenses are defenses of clay.

13Be silent, and I will speak.

Then let come to me what may.

14Why do I put myself at riska

and take my life in my own hands?

15Though Hehe slay me, I will hope in Himhim.b

I will still defend my ways to Hishis face.

16Moreover, this will be my salvation,

for no godless man can appear before Himhim.

17Listen carefully to my words;

let my declaration ring in your ears.

18Behold, now that I have prepared my case,

I know that I will be vindicated.

19Can anyone indict me?

If so, I will be silent and die.

20Only grant these two things to me,

so that I need not hide from Youyou:

21Withdraw Youryour hand from me,

and do not let Youryour terror frighten me.

22Then call me, and I will answer,

or let me speak, and Youyou can reply.

23How many are my iniquities and sins?

Reveal to me my transgression and sin.

24Why do Youyou hide Youryour face

and consider me as Youryour enemy?

25Would Youyou frighten a windblown leaf?

Would Youyou chase after dry chaff?

26For Youyou record bitter accusations against me

and bequeath to me the iniquities of my youth.

27You put my feet in the stocks

and stand watch over all my paths;

You set a limit

for the soles of my feet.

28So man wastes away like something rotten,

like a moth-eaten garment.
