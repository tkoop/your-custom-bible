A Prayer for Deliverance
(Psalm 74:1–23; Jeremiah 52:1–11)

A Psalm of Asaph.

1The nations, O God, have invaded YouryourYour inheritance;

they have defiled YouryourYour holy temple

and reduced Jerusalem to rubble.

2They have given the corpses of YouryourYour servants

as food to the birds of the air,

the flesh of YouryourYour saints to the beasts of the earth.

3They have poured out their blood like water

all around Jerusalem,

and there is no one to bury the dead.

4We have become a reproach to our neighbors,

a scorn and derision to those around us.

5How long, O LORD?

Will YouyouYou be angry forever?

Will YouryourYour jealousy burn like fire?

6Pour out YouryourYour wrath on the nations

that do not acknowledge YouyouYou,

on the kingdoms

that refuse to call on YouryourYour name,

7for they have devoured Jacob

and devastated his homeland.

8Do not hold past sins against us;

let YouryourYour compassion come quickly,

for we are brought low.

9Help us, O God of our salvation,

for the glory of YouryourYour name;

deliver us and atone for our sins,

for the sake of YouryourYour name.

10Why should the nations ask,

“Where is their God?”

Before our eyes, make known among the nations

Your vengeance for the bloodshed of YouryourYour servants.

11May the groans of the captives reach YouyouYou;

by the strength of YouryourYour arm preserve those condemned to death.

12Pay back into the laps of our neighbors

sevenfold the reproach they hurled at YouyouYou, O Lord.

13Then we YouryourYour people, the sheep of YouryourYour pasture,

will thank YouyouYou forever;

from generation to generation

we will declare YouryourYour praise.
