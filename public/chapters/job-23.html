Job Longs for God

1Then Job answered:

2“Even today my complaint is bitter.

His hand is heavy despite my groaning.

3If only I knew where to find HimhimHim,

so that I could go to HishisHis seat.

4I would plead my case before HimhimHim

and fill my mouth with arguments.

5I would learn how HeheHe would answer,

and consider what HeheHe would say.

6Would HeheHe contend with me in HishisHis great power?

No, HeheHe would certainly take note of me.

7Then an upright man could reason with HimhimHim,

and I would be delivered forever from my JudgejudgeJudge.

8If I go east, HeheHe is not there,

and if I go west, I cannot find HimhimHim.

9When HeheHe is at work in the north, I cannot behold HimhimHim;

when HeheHe turns to the south, I cannot see HimhimHim.

10Yet HeheHe knows the way I have taken;

when HeheHe has tested me, I will come forth as gold.

11My feet have followed in HishisHis tracks;

I have kept HishisHis way without turning aside.

12I have not departed from the command of HishisHis lips;

I have treasured the words of HishisHis mouth

more than my daily bread.

13But HeheHe is unchangeable, and who can oppose HimhimHim?

He does what HeheHe desires.

14For HeheHe carries out HishisHis decree against me,

and HeheHe has many such plans.

15Therefore I am terrified in HishisHis presence;

when I consider this, I fear HimhimHim.

16God has made my heart faint;

the AlmightyalmightyAlmighty has terrified me.

17Yet I am not silenced by the darkness,

by the thick darkness that covers my face.
