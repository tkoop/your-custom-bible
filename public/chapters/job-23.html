Job Longs for God

1Then Job answered:

2“Even today my complaint is bitter.

His hand is heavy despite my groaning.

3If only I knew where to find Himhim,

so that I could go to Hishis seat.

4I would plead my case before Himhim

and fill my mouth with arguments.

5I would learn how Hehe would answer,

and consider what Hehe would say.

6Would Hehe contend with me in Hishis great power?

No, Hehe would certainly take note of me.

7Then an upright man could reason with Himhim,

and I would be delivered forever from my Judgejudge.

8If I go east, Hehe is not there,

and if I go west, I cannot find Himhim.

9When Hehe is at work in the north, I cannot behold Himhim;

when Hehe turns to the south, I cannot see Himhim.

10Yet Hehe knows the way I have taken;

when Hehe has tested me, I will come forth as gold.

11My feet have followed in Hishis tracks;

I have kept Hishis way without turning aside.

12I have not departed from the command of Hishis lips;

I have treasured the words of Hishis mouth

more than my daily bread.

13But Hehe is unchangeable, and who can oppose Himhim?

He does what Hehe desires.

14For Hehe carries out Hishis decree against me,

and Hehe has many such plans.

15Therefore I am terrified in Hishis presence;

when I consider this, I fear Himhim.

16God has made my heart faint;

the Almightyalmighty has terrified me.

17Yet I am not silenced by the darkness,

by the thick darkness that covers my face.
