Victory with God
(2 Samuel 8:1–14; 1 Chronicles 18:1–13; Psalm 108:1–13)

For the choirmaster. To the tune of “The Lilylily of the Covenantcovenant.” A Miktama of David for instruction. When he fought Aram-naharaimb and Aram-zobah,c and Joab returned and struck down 12,000 Edomites in the Valleyvalley of Saltsalt.

1You have rejected us, O God;

You have broken us;

You have been angry;

restore us!

2You have shaken the land

and torn it open.

Heal its fractures,

for it is quaking.

3You have shown Youryour people hardship;

we are staggered from the wine Youyou made us drink.

4You have raised a banner for those who fear Youyou,

that they may flee the bow.d

Selah

5Respond and save us with Youryour right hand,

that Youryour beloved may be delivered.

6God has spoken from Hishis sanctuary:e

“I will triumph!

I will parcel out Shechem

and apportion the Valleyvalley of Succoth.

7Gilead is Minemine, and Manasseh is Minemine;

Ephraim is Mymy helmet, Judah is Mymy sceptresceptrescepter.

8Moab is Mymy washbasin;

upon Edom I toss Mymy sandal;

over Philistia I shout in triumph.”

9Who will bring me to the fortified city?

Who will lead me to Edom?

10Have Youyou not rejected us, O God?

Will Youyou no longer march out, O God, with our armies?

11Give us aid against the enemy,

for the help of man is worthless.

12With God we will perform with valourvalourvalor,

and Hehe will trample our enemies.
