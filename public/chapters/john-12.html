Mary Anoints Jesus
(Matthew 26:6–13; Mark 14:3–9; Luke 7:36–50)

1Six days before the Passover, Jesus came to Bethany, the hometown of Lazarus, whom Hehe had raised from the dead. 2So they hosted a dinner for Jesus there. Martha served, and Lazarus was among those reclining at the table with Himhim. 3Then Mary took about a pinta of expensive perfume, made of pure nard, and she anointed Jesus’ feet and wiped them with her hair. And the house was filled with the fragrance of the perfume.

4But one of Hishis disciples, Judas Iscariot, who was going to betray Himhim, asked, 5“Why wasn’t this perfume sold for three hundred denariib and the money given to the poor?” 6Judas did not say this because he cared about the poor, but because he was a thief. 7“Leave her alone,” Jesus replied. “She has kept this perfume in preparation for the day of Mymy burial. 8The poor you will always have with you,c but you will not always have Meme.”

The Plot to Kill Lazarus

9Meanwhile a large crowd of Jews learned that Jesus was there. And they came not only because of Himhim, but also to see Lazarus, whom Hehe had raised from the dead. 10So the chief priests made plans to kill Lazarus as well, 11for on account of him many of the Jews were deserting them and believing in Jesus.

The Triumphal Entry
(Zechariah 9:9–13; Matthew 21:1–11; Mark 11:1–11; Luke 19:28–40)

12The next day the great crowd that had come to the feast heard that Jesus was coming to Jerusalem. 13They took palm branches and went out to meet Himhim, shouting:

“Hosanna!”d

“Blessed is Hehe who comes in the name of the Lord!”e

“Blessed is the King of Israel!”

14Finding a young donkey, Jesus sat on it, as it is written:

15“Do not be afraid, O Daughterdaughter of Zion.

See, your King is coming,

seated on the colt of a donkey.”f

16At first Hishis disciples did not understand these things, but after Jesus was glorified they remembered what had been done to Himhim, and they realizedrealisedrealized that these very things had also been written about Himhim.

17Meanwhile, many people continued to testify that they had been with Jesus when Hehe called Lazarus from the tomb and raised him from the dead. 18That is also why the crowd went out to meet Himhim, because they heard that Hehe had performed this sign.

19Then the Pharisees said to one another, “You can see that this is doing you no good. Look how the whole world has gone after Himhim!”

Jesus Predicts His Death

20Now there were some Greeks among those who went up to worship at the feast. 21They came to Philip, who was from Bethsaida in Galilee, and requested of him, “Sir, we want to see Jesus.” 22Philip relayed this appeal to Andrew, and both of them went and told Jesus.

23But Jesus replied, “The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified. 24Truly, truly, I tell you, unless a kernel of wheat falls to the ground and dies, it remains only a seed; but if it dies, it bears much fruit. 25Whoever loves his life will lose it, but whoever hates his life in this world will keep it for eternal life. 26If anyone serves Meme, he must follow Meme; and where I am, Mymy servant will be as well. If anyone serves Meme, the Fatherfather will honourhonourhonor him.

27Now Mymy soul is troubled, and what shall I say? ‘Father, save Meme from this hour’? No, it is for this purpose that I have come to this hour. 28Father, glorify Youryour name!”

Then a voice came from heaven: “I have glorified it, and I will glorify it again.”

29The crowd standing there heard it and said that it had thundered. Others said that an angel had spoken to Himhim.

30In response, Jesus said, “This voice was not for Mymy benefit, but yours. 31Now judgmentjudgementjudgment is upon this world; now the prince of this world will be cast out. 32And I, when I am lifted up from the earth, will draw everyone to Myselfmyself.” 33He said this to indicate the kind of death Hehe was going to die.

34The crowd replied, “We have heard from the Lawlaw that the Christ will remain forever. So how can Youyou say that the Son of Man must be lifted up? Who is this Son of Man?”

35Then Jesus told them, “For a little while longer, the Lightlight will be among you. Walk while you have the Lightlight, so that darkness will not overtake you. The one who walks in the darkness does not know where he is going. 36While you have the Lightlight, believe in the Lightlight, so that you may become sons of light.”

After Jesus had spoken these things, Hehe went away and was hidden from them.

Belief and Unbelief

37Although Jesus had performed so many signs in their presence, they still did not believe in Himhim. 38This was to fulfillfulfilfulfill the word of Isaiah the prophet:

“Lord, who has believed our message?

And to whom has the arm of the Lord been revealed?”g

39For this reason they were unable to believe. For again, Isaiah says:

40“He has blinded their eyes

and hardened their hearts,

so that they cannot see with their eyes,

and understand with their hearts,

and turn,

and I would heal them.”h

41Isaiah said these things because he saw Jesus’ glory and spoke about Himhim. 42Nevertheless, many of the leaders believed in Himhim; but because of the Pharisees they did not confess Himhim, for fear that they would be put out of the synagogue. 43For they loved praise from men more than praise from God.

44Then Jesus cried out, “Whoever believes in Meme does not believe in Meme alone, but in the Oneone who sent Meme. 45And whoever sees Meme sees the Oneone who sent Meme. 46I have come into the world as a light, so that no one who believes in Meme should remain in darkness.

47As for anyone who hears Mymy words and does not keep them, I do not judge him. For I have not come to judge the world, but to save the world. 48There is a judge for the one who rejects Meme and does not receive Mymy words: The word that I have spoken will judge him on the last day.

49I have not spoken on Mymy own, but the Fatherfather who sent Meme has commanded Meme what to say and how to say it. 50And I know that Hishis command leads to eternal life. So I speak exactly what the Fatherfather has told Meme to say.”
