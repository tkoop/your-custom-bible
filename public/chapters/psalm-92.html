How Great Are Your Works!

A Psalm. A song for the Sabbath day.

1It is good to praise the LORD,

and to sing praises to YouryourYour name, O MostmostMost HighhighHigh,

2to proclaim YouryourYour loving devotion in the morning

and YouryourYour faithfulness at night

3with the ten-stringed harp

and the melody of the lyre.

4For YouyouYou, O LORD, have made me glad by YouryourYour deeds;

I sing for joy at the works of YouryourYour hands.

5How great are YouryourYour works, O LORD,

how deep are YouryourYour thoughts!

6A senseless man does not know,

and a fool does not understand,

7that though the wicked sprout like grass,

and all evildoers flourish,

they will be forever destroyed.

8But YouyouYou, O LORD, are exalted forever!

9For surely YouryourYour enemies, O LORD,

surely YouryourYour enemies will perish;

all evildoers will be scattered.

10But YouyouYou have exalted my horn like that of a wild ox;

with fine oil I have been anointed.

11My eyes see the downfall of my enemies;

my ears hear the wailing of my wicked foes.a

12The righteous will flourish like a palm tree,

and grow like a cedar in Lebanon.

13Planted in the house of the LORD,

they will flourish in the courts of our God.

14In old age they will still bear fruit;

healthy and green they will remain,

15to proclaim, “The LORD is upright; HeheHe is my RockrockRock,

and in HimhimHim there is no unrighteousness.”
