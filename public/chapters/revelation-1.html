Prologue
(Daniel 12:1–13)

1This is the revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave Himhim to show Hishis servants what must soona come to pass. He made it known by sending Hishis angel to Hishis servant John, 2who testifies to everything he saw. This is the word of God and the testimony of Jesus Christ.

3Blessed is the one who reads aloud the words of this prophecy, and blessed are those who hear and obey what is written in it, because the time is near.

John Greets the Seven Churches

4John,

To the seven churches in the province of Asia:b

Grace and peace to you from Himhim who is and was and is to come, and from the seven Spiritsspiritsc before Hishis throne, 5and from Jesus Christ, the faithful witness, the firstborn from the dead, and the ruler of the kings of the earth.

To Himhim who loves us and has released us from our sins by Hishis blood, 6who has made us to be a kingdom, priests to Hishis God and Fatherfather—to Himhim be the glory and power forever and ever! Amen.

7Behold, Hehe is coming with the clouds, and every eye will see Himhim—even those who pierced Himhim. And all the tribes of the earth will mourn because of Himhim. So shall it be! Amen.

8“I am the Alpha and the Omega,d” says the Lord God, who is and was and is to come—the Almightyalmighty.

John’s Vision on Patmos

9I, John, your brother and partner in the tribulation and kingdom and perseverance that are in Jesus, was on the island of Patmos because of the word of God and my testimony about Jesus. 10On the Lord’s day I was in the Spiritspirit, and I heard behind me a loud voice like a trumpet, 11saying,e “Write on a scroll what you see and send it to the seven churches: to Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea.”

12Then I turned to see the voice that was speaking with me. And having turned, I saw seven golden lampstands, 13and among the lampstands was Oneone like the Son of Man,f dressed in a long robe, with a golden sash around Hishis chest. 14The hair of Hishis head was white like wool, as white as snow, and Hishis eyes were like a blazing fire. 15His feet were like polished bronze refined in a furnace, and Hishis voice was like the roar of many waters. 16He held in Hishis right hand seven stars, and a sharp double-edged sword came from Hishis mouth. His face was like the sun shining at its brightest.

17When I saw Himhim, I fell at Hishis feet like a dead man. But Hehe placed Hishis right hand on me and said, “Do not be afraid. I am the Firstfirst and the Lastlast, 18the Living Oneone. I was dead, and behold, now I am alive forever and ever! And I hold the keys of Death and of Hades.

19Therefore write down the things you have seen, and the things that are, and the things that will happen after this. 20This is the mystery of the seven stars you saw in Mymy right hand and of the seven golden lampstands: The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches, and the seven lampstands are the seven churches.
