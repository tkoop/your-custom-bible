Christ’s Perfect Sacrifice
(Psalm 40:1–17)

1For the law is only a shadow of the good things to come, not the realities themselves. It can never, by the same sacrifices offered year after year, make perfect those who draw near to worship. 2If it could, would not the offerings have ceased? For the worshipers would have been cleansed once for all, and would no longer have felt the guilt of their sins.

3Instead, those sacrifices are an annual reminder of sins, 4because it is impossible for the blood of bulls and goats to take away sins. 5Therefore, when Christ came into the world, HeheHe said:

“Sacrifice and offering YouyouYou did not desire,

but a body YouyouYou prepared for MemeMe.

7Then I said, ‘Here I am, it is written about MemeMe in the scroll:

I have come to do YouryourYour will, O God.’ ”a

8In the passage above HeheHe says, “Sacrifices and offerings, burnt offerings and sin offerings YouyouYou did not desire, nor did YouyouYou delight in them” (although they are offered according to the law). 9Then HeheHe adds, “Here I am, I have come to do YouryourYour will.” He takes away the first to establish the second. 10And by that will, we have been sanctified through the sacrifice of the body of Jesus Christ once for all.

11Day after day every priest stands to minister and to offer again and again the same sacrifices, which can never take away sins. 12But when this Priest had offered for all time one sacrifice for sins, HeheHe sat down at the right hand of God. 13Since that time, HeheHe waits for HishisHis enemies to be made a footstool for HishisHis feet, 14because by a single offering HeheHe has made perfect for all time those who are being sanctified.

“Their sins and lawless acts

18And where these have been forgiven, an offering for sin is no longer needed.

A Call to Persevere

23Let us hold resolutely to the hope we profess, for HeheHe who promised is faithful. 24And let us consider how to spur one another on to love and good deeds. 25Let us not neglect meeting together, as some have made a habit, but let us encourage one another, and all the more as you see the DaydayDay approaching.
