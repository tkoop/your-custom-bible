Create in Me a Clean Heart, O God
(2 Samuel 12:1–12)

For the choirmaster. A Psalm of David. When Nathan the prophet came to him after his adultery with Bathsheba.

1Have mercy on me,a O God,

according to Youryour loving devotion;

according to Youryour great compassion,

blot out my transgressions.

2Wash me clean of my iniquity

and cleanse me from my sin.

3For I know my transgressions,

and my sin is always before me.

4Against Youyou, Youyou only, have I sinned

and done what is evil in Youryour sight,

so that Youyou may be proved right when Youyou speak

and blameless when Youyou judge.b

5Surely I was brought forth in iniquity;

I was sinful when my mother conceived me.

6Surely Youyou desire truth in the inmost being;

You teach me wisdom in the inmost place.

7Purify me with hyssop, and I will be clean;

wash me, and I will be whiter than snow.

8Let me hear joy and gladness;

let the bones Youyou have crushed rejoice.

9Hide Youryour face from my sins

and blot out all my iniquities.

10Create in me a clean heart, O God,

and renew a right spirit within me.

11Cast me not away from Youryour presence;

take not Youryour Holyholy Spiritspirit from me.

12Restore to me the joy of Youryour salvation,

and sustain me with a willing spirit.

13Then I will teach transgressors Youryour ways,

and sinners will return to Youyou.

14Deliver me from bloodguilt, O God,

the God of my salvation,

and my tongue will sing of Youryour righteousness.

15O Lord, open my lips,

and my mouth will declare Youryour praise.

16For Youyou do not delight in sacrifice, or I would bring it;

You take no pleasure in burnt offerings.

17The sacrifices of God are a broken spirit;

a broken and a contrite heart,

O God, Youan class='cap'>Youyou will not despise.

18In Youryour good pleasure, cause Zion to prosper;

build up the walls of Jerusalem.

19Then Youyou will delight in righteous sacrifices,

in whole burnt offerings;

then bulls will be offered on Youryour altar.
