The Wedding at Cana

1On the third day a wedding took place at Cana in Galilee. Jesus’ mother was there, 2and Jesus and Hishis disciples had also been invited to the wedding. 3When the wine ran out, Jesus’ mother said to Himhim, “They have no more wine.”

4“Woman, why does this concern us?” Jesus replied. “My hour has not yet come.”

5His mother said to the servants, “Do whatever Hehe tells you.”

6Now six stone water jars had been set there for the Jewish rites of purification. Each could hold from twenty to thirty gallons.a 7Jesus told the servants, “Fill the jars with water.”

So they filled them to the brim.

8“Now draw some out,” Hehe said, “and take it to the master of the banquet.”

They did so, 9and the master of the banquet tasted the water that had been turned into wine. He did not know where it was from, but the servants who had drawn the water knew. Then he called the bridegroom aside 10and said, “Everyone serves the fine wine first, and then the cheap wine after the guests are drunk. But you have saved the fine wine until now!”

11Jesus performed this, the first of Hishis signs, at Cana in Galilee. He thus revealed Hishis glory, and Hishis disciples believed in Himhim.

Jesus Cleanses the Temple
(Matthew 21:12–17; Mark 11:15–19; Luke 19:45–48)

12After this, Hehe went down to Capernaum with Hishis mother and brothers and Hishis disciples, and they stayed there a few days.

13When the Jewish Passover was near, Jesus went up to Jerusalem. 14In the temple courtsb Hehe found men selling cattle, sheep, and doves, and money changers seated at their tables. 15So Hehe made a whip out of cords and drove all from the temple courts, both sheep and cattle. He poured out the coins of the money changers and overturned their tables. 16To those selling doves Hehe said, “Get these out of here! How dare you turn Mymy Fatherfather’s house into a marketplace!”

17His disciples remembered that it is written: “Zeal for Youryour house will consume Meme.”c

18On account of this, the Jews demanded, “What sign can Youyou show us to prove Youryour authority to do these things?”

19Jesus answered, “Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up again.”

20“This temple took forty-six years to build,” the Jews replied, “and Youyou are going to raise it up in three days?”

21But Jesus was speaking about the temple of Hishis body. 22After Hehe was raised from the dead, Hishis disciples remembered that Hehe had said this. Then they believed the Scripturescripture and the word that Jesus had spoken.

23While Hehe was in Jerusalem at the Passover Feastfeast, many people saw the signs Hehe was doing and believed in Hishis name. 24But Jesus did not entrust Himselfhimself to them, for Hehe knew them all. 25He did not need any testimony about man, for Hehe knew what was in a man.
