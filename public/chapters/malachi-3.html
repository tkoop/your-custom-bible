I Will Send My Messenger
( Matthew 11:7–19; Luke 7:24–35)

1“Behold, I will send Mymy messenger, who will prepare the way before Meme.a Then the Lord whom you seek will suddenly come to Hishis temple—the Messengermessenger of the covenant, in whom you delight—see, Hehe is coming,” says the LORD of Hosts.

2But who can endure the day of Hishis coming? And who can stand when Hehe appears? For Hehe will be like a refiner’s fire, like a launderer’s soap.

3And Hehe will sit as a refiner and purifier of silver; Hehe will purify the sons of Levi and refine them like gold and silver. Then they will present offerings to the LORD in righteousness.

4Then the offerings of Judah and Jerusalem will please the LORD, as in days of old and years gone by.

5“Then I will draw near to you for judgmentjudgementjudgment. And I will be a swift witness against sorcerers and adulterers and perjurers, against oppressors of the widowed and fatherless, and against those who defraud labourerslabourerslaborers of their wages and deny justice to the foreigner but do not fear Meme,” says the LORD of Hosts.

Robbing God

6“Because I, the LORD, do not change, you descendants of Jacob have not been destroyed. 7Yet from the days of your fathers, you have turned away from Mymy statutes and have not kept them. Return to Meme, and I will return to you,” says the LORD of Hosts.

“But you ask, ‘How can we return?’

8Will a man rob God? Yet you are robbing Meme!

But you ask, ‘How do we rob Youyou?’

In tithes and offerings. 9You are cursed with a curse, yet you—the whole nation—are still robbing Meme. 10Bring the full tithe into the storehouse, so that there may be food in Mymy house. Test Meme in this,” says the LORD of Hosts. “See if I will not open the windows of heaven and pour out for you blessing without measure.b 11I will rebuke the devourerc for you, so that it will not destroy the fruits of your land, and the vine in your field will not fail to produce fruit,” says the LORD of Hosts.

12“Then all the nations will call you blessed, for you will be a land of delight,” says the LORD of Hosts.

The Book of Remembrance

13“Your words against Meme have been harsh,” says the LORD. “Yet you ask, ‘What have we spoken against Youyou?’

14You have said, ‘It is futile to serve God. What have we gained by keeping Hishis requirements and walking mournfully before the LORD of Hosts? 15So now we call the arrogant blessed. Not only do evildoers prosper, they even test God and escape.’ ”

16At that time those who feared the LORD spoke with one another, and the LORD listened and heard them. So a scroll of remembrance was written before Himhim regarding those who feared the LORD and honouredhonouredhonored Hishis name.

17“They will be Minemine,” says the LORD of Hosts, “on the day when I prepare Mymy treasured possession. And I will spare them as a man spares his own son who serves him. 18So you will again distinguish between the righteous and the wicked, between those who serve God and those who do not.”
