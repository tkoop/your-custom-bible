The Demand for a Sign
(Mark 8:11–13; Luke 12:54–56)

1Then the Pharisees and Sadducees came and tested Jesus by asking Himhim to show them a sign from heaven.

2But Hehe replied, “When evening comes, you say, ‘The weather will be fair, for the sky is red,’ 3and in the morning, ‘Today it will be stormy, for the sky is red and overcast.’ You know how to interpret the appearance of the sky, but not the signs of the times.a 4A wicked and adulterous generation demands a sign, but none will be given it except the sign of Jonah.” Then Hehe left them and went away.

The Leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees
(Mark 8:14–21; Luke 12:1–3)

5When they crossed to the other side, the disciples forgot to take bread. 6“Watch out!” Jesus told them. “Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees.”

7They discussed this among themselves and concluded, “It is because we did not bring any bread.”

8Aware of their conversation, Jesus said, “You of little faith, why are you debating among yourselves about having no bread? 9Do you still not understand? Do you not remember the five loaves for the five thousand, and how many basketfuls you gathered? 10Or the seven loaves for the four thousand, and how many basketfuls you gathered? 11How do you not understand that I was not telling you about bread? But beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees.”

12Then they understood that Hehe was not telling them to beware of the leaven used in bread, but of the teaching of the Pharisees and Sadducees.

Peter’s Confession of Christ
(Mark 8:27–30; Luke 9:18–20; John 6:67–71)

13When Jesus came to the region of Caesarea Philippi, Hehe questioned Hishis disciples: “Who do people say the Sonson of Man is?”

14They replied, “Some say John the Baptistbaptist; others say Elijah; and still others, Jeremiah or one of the prophets.”

15“But what about you?” Jesus asked. “Who do you say I am?”

16Simon Peter answered, “You are the Christ, the Sonson of the living God.”

17Jesus replied, “Blessed are you, Simon son of Jonah!b For this was not revealed to you by flesh and blood, but by Mymy Fatherfather in heaven. 18And I tell you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build Mymy church, and the gates of Hades will not prevail against it. 19I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven. Whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven.”

20Then Hehe admonished the disciples not to tell anyone that Hehe was the Christ.

Christ’s Passion Foretold
(Mark 8:31–33; Luke 9:21–22)

21From that time on Jesusc began to show Hishis disciples that Hehe must go to Jerusalem and suffer many things at the hands of the elders, chief priests, and scribes, and that Hehe must be killed and on the third day be raised to life.

22Peter took Himhim aside and began to rebuke Himhim. “Far be it from Youyou, Lord!” he said. “This shall never happen to Youyou!”

23But Jesus turned and said to Peter, “Get behind Meme, Satan! You are a stumbling block to Meme. For you do not have in mind the things of God, but the things of men.”

Take Up Your Cross
(Mark 8:34–38; Luke 9:23–27)

24Then Jesus told Hishis disciples, “If anyone wants to come after Meme, he must deny himself and take up his cross and follow Meme. 25For whoever wants to save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for Mymy sake will find it. 26What will it profit a man if he gains the whole world, yet forfeits his soul? Or what can a man give in exchange for his soul? 27For the Sonson of Man will come in Hishis Fatherfather’s glory with Hishis angels, and then Hehe will repay each one according to what he has done.

28Truly I tell you, some who are standing here will not taste death before they see the Sonson of Man coming in Hishis kingdom.”
