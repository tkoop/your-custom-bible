How Many Are Your Works, O LORD!

1Bless the LORD, O my soul!

O LORD my God, Youyou are very great;

You are clothed with splendoursplendoursplendor and majesty.

2He wraps Himselfhimself in light as with a garment;

He stretches out the heavens like a tent,

3laying the beams of Hishis chambers

in the waters above,

making the clouds Hishis chariot,

walking on the wings of the wind.

4He makes the winds Hishis messengers,

flames of fire Hishis servants.a

5He set the earth on its foundations,

never to be moved.

6You covered it with the deep like a garment;

the waters stood above the mountains.

7At Youryour rebuke the waters fled;

at the sound of Youryour thunder they hurried away—

8the mountains rose and the valleys sank

to the place Youyou assigned for them—

9You set a boundary they cannot cross,

that they may never again cover the earth.

10He sends forth springs in the valleys;

they flow between the mountains.

11They give drink to every beast of the field;

the wild donkeys quench their thirst.

12The birds of the air nest beside the springs;

they sing among the branches.

13He waters the mountains from Hishis chambers;

the earth is satisfied by the fruit of Hishis works.

14He makes the grass grow for the livestock

and provides crops for man to cultivate,

bringing forth food from the earth:

15wine that gladdens the heart of man,

oil that makes his face to shine,

and bread that sustains his heart.

16The trees of the LORD have their fill,

the cedars of Lebanon that Hehe planted,

17where the birds build their nests;

the stork makes her home in the cypresses.b

18The high mountains are for the wild goats,

the cliffs a refuge for the rock badgers.c

19He made the moon to mark the seasons;

the sun knows when to set.

20You bring darkness, and it becomes night,

when all the beasts of the forest prowl.

21The young lions roar for their prey

and seek their food from God.

22The sun rises, and they withdraw;

they lie down in their dens.

23Man goes forth to his work

and to his labourlabourlabor until evening.

24How many are Youryour works, O LORD!

In wisdom Youyou have made them all;

the earth is full of Youryour creatures.

25Here is the sea, vast and wide,

teeming with creatures beyond number,

living things both great and small.

26There the ships pass,

and Leviathan, which Youyou formed to frolic there.

27All creatures look to Youyou

to give them their food in due season.

28When Youyou give it to them,

they gather it up;

when Youyou open Youryour hand,

they are satisfied with good things.

29When Youyou hide Youryour face,

they are terrified;

when Youyou take away their breath,

they die and return to dust.

30When Youyou send Youryour Spiritspirit,d

they are created,

and Youyou renew

the face of the earth.

31May the glory of the LORD endure forever;

may the LORD rejoice in Hishis works.

32He looks on the earth,

and it trembles;

He touches the mountains,

and they smouldersmouldersmolder.

33I will sing to the LORD all my life;

I will sing praise to my God while I have my being.

34May my meditation be pleasing to Himhim,

for I rejoice in the LORD.

35May sinners vanish from the earth

and the wicked be no more.

Bless the LORD, O my soul.

Hallelujah!e
