In You My Soul Takes Refuge
(1 Samuel 22:1–5; Psalm 108:1–13; Psalm 142:1–7)

For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Notnot Destroydestroy.” A Miktama of David, when he fled from Saul into the cave.

1Have mercy on me, O God, have mercy,

for in Youyou my soul takes refuge.

In the shadow of Youryour wings I will take shelter

until the danger has passed.

2I cry out to God Mostmost Highhigh,b

to God who fulfills Hishis purpose for me.

3He reaches down from heaven and saves me;

He rebukes those who trample me.

Selah

God sends forth

His loving devotion and Hishis truth.

4My soul is among the lions;

I lie down with ravenous beasts—

with men whose teeth are spears and arrows,

whose tongues are sharp swords.

5Be exalted, O God, above the heavens;

may Youryour glory cover all the earth.

6They spread a net for my feet;

my soul was despondent.

They dug a pit before me,

but they themselves have fallen into it!

Selah

7My heart is steadfast, O God, my heart is steadfast.

I will sing and make music.

8Awake, my glory!c

Awake, O harp and lyre!

I will awaken the dawn.

9I will praise Youyou, O Lord, among the nations;

I will sing Youryour praises among the peoples.

10For Youryour loving devotion reaches to the heavens,

and Youryour faithfulness to the clouds.

11Be exalted, O God, above the heavens;

may Youryour glory cover all the earth.

 

 

Footnotes:

 

 

1 a Miktam is probably a musical or liturgical term; used for Psalms 16 and 56–60.

 

2 b Hebrew Elohim-Elyon

 

8 c Or Awake, my soul!
