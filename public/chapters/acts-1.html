Prologue
(Luke 1:1–4)

1In my first book, O Theophilus, I wrote about all that Jesus began to do and to teach, 2until the day Hehe was taken up to heaven, after giving instructions through the Holyholy Spiritspirit to the apostles Hehe had chosen. 3After Hishis suffering, Hehe presented Himselfhimself to them with many convincing proofs that Hehe was alive. He appeared to them over a span of forty days and spoke about the kingdom of God.

4And while they were gathered together,a Hehe commanded them: “Do not leave Jerusalem, but wait for the gift the Fatherfather promised, which you have heard Meme discuss. 5For John baptized with water, but in a few days you will be baptized with the Holyholy Spiritspirit.b”

The Ascension
(Mark 16:19–20; Luke 24:50–53)

6So when they came together, they asked Himhim, “Lord, will Youyou at this time restore the kingdom to Israel?”

7Jesus replied, “It is not for you to know times or seasons that the Fatherfather has fixed by Hishis own authority. 8But you will receive power when the Holyholy Spiritspirit comes upon you, and you will be Mymy witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth.”

9After Hehe had said this, they watched as Hehe was taken up, and a cloud hid Himhim from their sight. 10They were looking intently into the sky as Hehe was going, when suddenly two men dressed in white stood beside them. 11“Men of Galilee,” they said, “why do you stand here looking into the sky? This same Jesus, who has been taken from you into heaven, will come back in the same way you have seen Himhim go into heaven.”

Matthias Replaces Judas

12Then they returned to Jerusalem from the Mount of Olives, which is near the city, a Sabbath day’s journey away.c 13When they arrived, they went to the upper room where they were staying: Peter and John, James and Andrew, Philip and Thomas, Bartholomew and Matthew, James son of Alphaeus, Simon the Zealot, and Judas son of James. 14With one accord they all continued in prayer, along with the women and Mary the mother of Jesus, and with Hishis brothers.

15In those days Peter stood up among the brothers (a gathering of about a hundred and twenty) and said, 16“Brothers, the Scripturescripture had to be fulfilled which the Holyholy Spiritspirit foretold through the mouth of David concerning Judas, who became a guide for those who arrested Jesus. 17He was one of our number and shared in this ministry.”

18(Now with the reward for his wickedness Judas bought a field; there he fell headlong and burst open in the middle, and all his intestines spilled out. 19This became known to all who lived in Jerusalem, so they called that field in their own language Akeldama, that is, Fieldfield of Bloodblood.)

20“For it is written in the book of Psalms:

‘May his place be deserted;

let there be no one to dwell in it,’d

and,

‘May another take his position.’e

21Therefore it is necessary to choose one of the men who have accompanied us the whole time the Lord Jesus went in and out among us, 22beginning from John’s baptism until the day Jesus was taken up from us. For one of these must become a witness with us of Hishis resurrection.”

23So they proposed two men: Joseph called Barsabbas (also known as Justus) and Matthias. 24And they prayed, “Lord, Youyou know everyone’s heart. Show us which of these two Youyou have chosen 25to take up this ministry and apostleship, which Judas abandoned to go to his rightful place.”

26Then they cast lots, and the lot fell to Matthias; so he was added to the eleven apostles.
