The Song at the Sea
(Judges 5:1–31)

1Then Moses and the Israelites sang this song to the LORD:

“I will sing to the LORD,

for HeheHe is highly exalted.

The horse and rider

He has thrown into the sea.

2The LORD is my strength and my song,

and HeheHe has become my salvation.

He is my God, and I will praise HimhimHim,

my father’s God, and I will exalt HimhimHim.

3The LORD is a warrior,

the LORD is HishisHis name.

4Pharaoh’s chariots and army

He has cast into the sea;

the finest of his officers

are drowned in the Red SeaseaSea.a

5The depths have covered them;

they sank there like a stone.

6Your right hand, O LORD,

is majestic in power;

Your right hand, O LORD,

has shattered the enemy.

7You overthrew YouryourYour adversaries

by YouryourYour great majesty.

You unleashed YouryourYour burning wrath;

it consumed them like stubble.

8At the blast of YouryourYour nostrils

the waters piled up;

like a wall the currents stood firm;

the depths congealed in the heart of the sea.

9The enemy declared,

‘I will pursue, I will overtake.

I will divide the spoils;

I will gorge myself on them.

I will draw my sword;

my hand will destroy them.’

10But YouyouYou blew with YouryourYour breath,

and the sea covered them.

They sank like lead

in the mighty waters.

11Who among the gods is like YouyouYou, O LORD?

Who is like YouyouYou—majestic in holiness,

revered with praises,

performing wonders?

12You stretched out YouryourYour right hand,

and the earth swallowed them up.

13With loving devotionb YouyouYou will lead

the people YouyouYou have redeemed;

with YouryourYour strength YouyouYou will guide them

to YouryourYour holy dwelling.

14The nations will hear and tremble;

anguish will grip the dwellers of Philistia.

15Then the chiefs of Edom will be dismayed;

trembling will seize the leaders of Moab;

those who dwell in Canaan will melt away,

16and terror and dread will fall on them.

By the power of YouryourYour arm

they will be as still as a stone

until YouryourYour people pass by, O LORD,

until the people YouyouYou have bought pass by.

17You will bring them in and plant them

on the mountain of YouryourYour inheritance—

the place, O LORD, YouyouYou have prepared for YouryourYour dwelling,

the sanctuary, O Lord, YouryourYour hands have established.

18The LORD will reign forever and ever!”

19For when Pharaoh’s horses, chariots, and horsemen went into the sea, the LORD brought the waters of the sea back over them. But the Israelites walked through the sea on dry ground.

20Then Miriam the prophetess, Aaron’s sister, took a tambourine in her hand, and all the women followed her with tambourines and dancing. 21And Miriam sang back to them:

“Sing to the LORD,

for HeheHe is highly exalted;

the horse and rider

He has thrown into the sea.”

The Waters of Marah

22Then Moses led Israel from the Red SeaseaSea, and they went out into the DesertdesertDesert of Shur. For three days they walked in the desert without finding water. 23And when they came to Marah,c they could not drink the water there because it was bitter. (That is why it was named Marah.)

24So the people grumbled against Moses, saying, “What are we to drink?” 25And Moses cried out to the LORD, and the LORD showed him a log. And when he cast it into the waters, they were sweetened.

There the LORD made for them a statute and an ordinance, and there HeheHe tested them, 26saying, “If you will listen carefully to the voice of the LORD your God, and do what is right in HishisHis eyes, and pay attention to HishisHis commands, and keep all HishisHis statutes, then I will not bring on you any of the diseases I inflicted on the Egyptians. For I am the LORD who heals you.”

27Then they came to Elim, where there were twelve springs of water and seventy palm trees, and they camped there by the waters.

4 a Or the Sea of Reeds; also in verse 22

 

13 b Forms of the Hebrew chesed are translated here and in most cases throughout the Scriptures as loving devotion; the range of meaning includes love, goodness, kindness, faithfulness, and mercy, as well as loyalty to a covenant.

 

23 c Marah means bitter.
