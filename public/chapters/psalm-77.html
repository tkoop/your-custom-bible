In the Day of Trouble I Sought the LORD

For the choirmaster. According to Jeduthun. A Psalm of Asaph.

1I cried out to God;

I cried aloud to God to hear me.

2In the day of trouble I sought the Lord;

through the night my outstretched hands did not grow weary;

my soul refused to be comforted.

3I remembered YouyouYou, O God, and I groaned;

I mused and my spirit grew faint.

Selah

4You have kept my eyes from closing;

I am too troubled to speak.

5I considered the days of old,

the years long in the past.

6At night I remembered my song;

in my heart I mused, and my spirit pondered:

7“Will the Lord spurn us forever

and never show HishisHis favourfavourfavor again?

8Is HishisHis loving devotion gone forever?

Has HishisHis promise failed for all time?

9Has God forgotten to be gracious?

Has HishisHis anger shut off HishisHis compassion?”

Selah

and ponder YouryourYour mighty deeds.

13Your way, O God, is holy.

What god is so great as our God?

14You are the God who works wonders;

You display YouryourYour strength among the peoples.

15With power YouyouYou redeemed YouryourYour people,

16The waters saw YouyouYou, O God;

17The clouds poured down water;

18Your thunder resounded in the whirlwind;

19Your path led through the sea,

Your way through the mighty waters,

but YouryourYour footprints were not to be found.b

20You led YouryourYour people like a flock

by the hand of Moses and Aaron.
