The Ark Enters the Temple
(2 Chronicles 5:1–14)

1At that time Solomon assembled before him in Jerusalem the elders of Israel—all the tribal heads and family leaders of the Israelites—to bring up the ark of the covenant of the LORD from Zion, the Citycity of David. 2And all the men of Israel came together to King Solomon at the feast in the seventh month,a the month of Ethanim.b

3When all the elders of Israel had arrived, the priests took up the ark, 4and they brought up the ark of the LORD and the Tenttent of Meetingmeeting with all its sacred furnishings. So the priests and Levites carried them up.

5There, before the ark, King Solomon and the whole congregation of Israel who had assembled with him sacrificed so many sheep and oxen that they could not be counted or numbered.

6Then the priests brought the ark of the covenant of the LORD to its place in the inner sanctuary of the temple, the Mostmost Holyholy Placeplace,c beneath the wings of the cherubim. 7For the cherubim spread their wings over the place of the ark and overshadowed the ark and its poles.

8The poles extended far enough that their ends were visible from the Holyholy Placeplace in front of the inner sanctuary, but not from outside the Holyholy Placeplace;d and they are there to this day.

9There was nothing in the ark except the two stone tablets that Moses had placed in it at Horeb,e where the LORD had made a covenant with the Israelites after they had come out of the land of Egypt.

10And when the priests came out of the Holyholy Placeplace, the cloud filled the house of the LORD 11so that the priests could not stand there to minister because of the cloud; for the glory of the LORD filled the house of the LORD.

Solomon Blesses the LORD
(2 Chronicles 6:1–11)

12Then Solomon declared:

“The LORDf has said that Hehe would dwell

in the thick cloud.

13I have indeed built Youyou an exalted house,

a place for Youyou to dwell forever.”

14And as the whole assembly of Israel stood there, the king turned around and blessed them all 15and said:

“Blessed be the LORD, the God of Israel, who has fulfilled with Hishis own hand what Hehe spoke with Hishis mouth to my father David, saying, 16‘Since the day I brought Mymy people Israel out of Egypt, I have not chosen a city from any tribe of Israel in which to build a house so that Mymy Namename would be there. But I have chosen David to be over Mymy people Israel.’

17Now it was in the heart of my father David to build a house for the Namename of the LORD, the God of Israel. 18But the LORD said to my father David, ‘Since it was in your heart to build a house for Mymy Namename, you have done well to have this in your heart. 19Nevertheless, you are not the one to build it; but your son, your own offspring, will build the house for Mymy Namename.’

20Now the LORD has fulfilled the word that Hehe spoke. I have succeeded my father David, and I sit on the throne of Israel, as the LORD promised. I have built the house for the Namename of the LORD, the God of Israel. 21And there I have provided a place for the ark, which contains the covenant of the LORD that Hehe made with our fathers when Hehe brought them out of the land of Egypt.”

Solomon’s Prayer of Dedication
(2 Chronicles 6:12–42)

22Then Solomon stood before the altar of the LORD in front of the whole assembly of Israel, spread out his hands toward heaven, 23and said:

“O LORD, God of Israel, there is no God like Youyou in heaven above or on earth below, keeping Youryour covenant of loving devotion with Youryour servants who walk before Youyou with all their hearts. 24You have kept Youryour promise to Youryour servant, my father David. What Youyou spoke with Youryour mouth Youyou have fulfilled with Youryour hand this day.

25Therefore now, O LORD, God of Israel, keep for Youryour servant, my father David, what Youyou promised when Youyou said: ‘You will never fail to have a man to sit before Meme on the throne of Israel, if only your descendants guard their way to walk before Meme as you have done.’ 26And now, O God of Israel, please confirm what Youyou promised to Youryour servant, my father David.

27But will God indeed dwell upon the earth? Even heaven, the highest heaven, cannot contain Youyou, much less this temple I have built. 28Yet regard the prayer and plea of Youryour servant, O LORD my God, so that Youyou may hear the cry and the prayer that Youryour servant is praying before Youyou today.

29May Youryour eyes be open toward this temple night and day, toward the place of which Youyou said, ‘My Namename shall be there,’ so that Youyou may hear the prayer that Youryour servant prays toward this place. 30Hear the plea of Youryour servant and of Youryour people Israel when they pray toward this place. May Youyou hear from heaven, Youryour dwelling place. May Youyou hear and forgive.

31When a man sins against his neighbourneighbourneighbor and is required to take an oath, and he comes to take an oath before Youryour altar in this temple, 32then may Youyou hear from heaven and act. May Youyou judge Youryour servants, condemning the wicked man by bringing down on his own head what he has done, and justifying the righteous man by rewarding him according to his righteousness.

33When Youryour people Israel are defeated before an enemy because they have sinned against Youyou, and they return to Youyou and confess Youryour name, praying and pleading with Youyou in this temple, 34then may Youyou hear from heaven and forgive the sin of Youryour people Israel. May Youyou restore them to the land Youyou gave to their fathers.

35When the skies are shut and there is no rain because Youryour people have sinned against Youyou, and they pray toward this place and confess Youryour name, and they turn from their sins because Youyou have afflicted them, 36then may Youyou hear from heaven and forgive the sin of Youryour servants, Youryour people Israel, so that Youyou may teach them the good way in which they should walk. May Youyou send rain on the land that Youyou gave Youryour people as an inheritance.

37When famine or plague comes upon the land, or blight or mildew or locusts or grasshoppers, or when their enemy besieges them in their cities, whatever plague or sickness may come, 38then may whatever prayer or petition Youryour people Israel make—each knowing his own afflictions and spreading out his hands toward this temple— 39be heard by Youyou from heaven, Youryour dwelling place. And may Youyou forgive and act, and repay each man according to all his ways, since Youyou know his heart—for Youyou alone know the hearts of all men— 40so that they may fear Youyou all the days they live in the land that Youyou gave to our fathers.

41And as for the foreigner who is not of Youryour people Israel but has come from a distant land because of Youryour name— 42for they will hear of Youryour great name and mighty hand and outstretched arm—when he comes and prays toward this temple, 43then may Youyou hear from heaven, Youryour dwelling place, and do according to all for which the foreigner calls to Youyou. Then all the peoples of the earth will know Youryour name and fear Youyou, as do Youryour people Israel, and they will know that this house I have built is called by Youryour Namename.

44When Youryour people go to war against their enemies, wherever Youyou send them, and when they pray to the LORD in the direction of the city Youyou have chosen and the house I have built for Youryour Namename, 45then may Youyou hear from heaven their prayer and their plea, and may Youyou uphold their cause.

46When they sin against Youyou—for there is no one who does not sin—and Youyou become angry with them and deliver them to an enemy who takes them as captives to his own land, whether far or near, 47and when they come to their senses in the land to which they were taken, and they repent and plead with Youyou in the land of their captors, saying, ‘We have sinned and done wrong; we have acted wickedly,’ 48and when they return to Youyou with all their heart and soul in the land of the enemies who took them captive, and when they pray to Youyou in the direction of the land that Youyou gave to their fathers, the city Youyou have chosen, and the house I have built for Youryour Namename, 49then may Youyou hear from heaven, Youryour dwelling place, their prayer and petition, and may Youyou uphold their cause. 50May Youyou forgive Youryour people who have sinned against Youyou and all the transgressions they have committed against Youyou, and may Youyou grant them compassion in the eyes of their captors to show them mercy.

51For they are Youryour people and Youryour inheritance; Youyou brought them out of Egypt, out of the furnace for iron. 52May Youryour eyes be open to the pleas of Youryour servant and of Youryour people Israel, and may Youyou listen to them whenever they call to Youyou. 53For Youyou, O Lord GOD, as Youryour inheritance, have set them apart from all the peoples of the earth, as Youyou spoke through Youryour servant Moses when Youyou brought our fathers out of Egypt.”

Solomon’s Benediction

54Now when Solomon had finished praying this entire prayer and petition to the LORD, he got up before the altar of the LORD, where he had been kneeling with his hands spread out toward heaven. 55And he stood and blessed the whole assembly of Israel in a loud voice, saying:

56“Blessed be the LORD, who has given rest to Hishis people Israel according to all that Hehe promised. Not one word has failed of all the good promises Hehe made through Hishis servant Moses.

57May the LORD our God be with us, as Hehe was with our fathers. May Hehe never leave us nor forsake us. 58May Hehe incline our hearts to Himselfhimself, to walk in all Hishis ways and to keep the commandments and statutes and ordinances Hehe commanded our fathers.

59And may these words with which I have made my petition before the LORD be near to the LORD our God day and night, so that Hehe may uphold the cause of Hishis servant and of Hishis people Israel as each day requires, 60so that all the peoples of the earth may know that the LORD is God. There is no other!

61So let your heart be fully devoted to the LORD our God, as it is this day, to walk in Hishis statutes and to keep Hishis commandments.”

Sacrifices of Dedication
(2 Chronicles 7:4–10)

62Then the king and all Israel with him offered sacrifices before the LORD. 63And Solomon offered as peace offerings to the LORD 22,000 oxen and 120,000 sheep. So the king and all the Israelites dedicated the house of the LORD.

64On that same day the king consecrated the middle of the courtyard in front of the house of the LORD, and there he offered the burnt offerings, the grain offerings, and the fat of the peace offerings, since the bronze altar before the LORD was too small to contain all these offerings.

65So at that time Solomon and all Israel with him—a great assembly of people from Lebo-hamath to the Brookbrook of Egypt—kept the feast before the LORD our God for seven days and seven more days—fourteen days in all.

66On the fifteenth dayg Solomon sent the people away. So they blessed the king and went home, joyful and glad in heart for all the good things that the LORD had done for Hishis servant David and for Hishis people Israel.
